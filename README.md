# HR Analytics Dashboard – Headcount, Retention & Turnover

An interactive multi-page Power BI report designed to analyze workforce composition, employee retention, and turnover trends across departments, locations, and demographics.



## Purpose

The HR Analytics Dashboard is a Power BI report built to analyze organizational workforce data over a **7-year period (2013–2019)**. It focuses on headcount distribution, employee retention behavior, and turnover drivers to support strategic HR decision-making.



## Tech Stack

The report was built using the following tools and technologies:

- **Power BI Desktop** – Data modeling, visualization, and report development  
- **DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)** – KPI calculations for Headcount, Retention %, Turnover %, salary rankings, and time-based analysis  
- **Data Modeling** – Star schema with fact and dimension tables  
- **PowerPoint** – Custom background and UI design  



## Data Source

- **Data Origin:** Combination of GPT-generated synthetic data and Kaggle datasets  

- **Dataset Structure & Size:**  
  - Date Dimension: **40,178 rows × 10 columns**  
  - Employee Dimension: **4,139 rows × 9 columns**  
  - Employee Transaction Fact Table: **4,139 rows × 16 columns**
    


## Features & Highlights

### Business Problem

HR teams often struggle to analyze workforce trends, attrition risks, and demographic distributions using raw HR data spread across multiple tables.

### Goals of the Report

To deliver an interactive HR analytics report that:

- Tracks headcount growth and workforce composition  
- Analyzes employee retention and turnover trends  
- Identifies high-risk departments, job levels, and termination drivers  
- Enables intuitive navigation and exploration for stakeholders
  

## Walkthrough of Key Pages

### Headcount
- Total headcount KPI with time-based filtering  
- Distribution by department, job level, location, and demographics  
- Custom tooltips highlighting Top/Bottom salary earners by department and city  

### Retention
- Retention % KPI with year-over-year trends  
- Retention analysis by department, job level, and education  
- Visual comparison of starting vs ending headcount  

### Turnover
- Turnover % KPI with trend analysis  
- Turnover breakdown by department and job level  
- Voluntary vs involuntary exits and termination reasons
